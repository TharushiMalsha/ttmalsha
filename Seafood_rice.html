Seafood Rice

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati or any fragrant rice
  • 250g mixed seafood (prawns, squid, fish, mussels)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green chili, sliced
  • 1 red chili, sliced
  • 2 sprigs curry leaves
  • 1-inch piece pandan leaf (optional)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 cardamom pods
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 2 cups seafood or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Cooking Instructions

Prepare Rice:

  • Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.

Prepare Seafood:

  • Clean and devein the prawns. Cut fish and squid into bite-sized pieces. If using mussels, clean and debeard them.

Sauté Aromatics:

  • In a large pan or pot, heat coconut oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, fennel seeds, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and cloves. Sauté until the spices release their aroma.

Add Onions and Curry Leaves:

  • Add chopped onions, curry leaves, and pandan leaf (if using). Sauté until the onions are golden brown.

Add Ginger-Garlic Paste and Chilies:

  • Stir in ginger-garlic paste and sliced green and red chilies. Cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

Add Tomatoes and Spices:

  • Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, chili powder, and coriander powder. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil begins to separate.

Add Seafood:

  • Add the mixed seafood to the pan and cook for a few minutes until they start to change color.

Add Rice and Coconut Milk:

  • Add the soaked and drained rice to the pan. Stir well to coat the rice in the spices. Pour in coconut milk and mix.

Pour in Broth:

  • Add seafood or vegetable broth to the pan. Season with salt according to your taste. Bring the mixture to a boil.

Simmer and Cook:

  • Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed the liquid.

Check Seafood:

  • Gently stir the rice, ensuring the seafood is evenly distributed. Cover and let it cook until the seafood is fully cooked.

Garnish and Serve:

  • Once done, garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with lime wedges on the side.
