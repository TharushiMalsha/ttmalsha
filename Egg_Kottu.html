Egg Kottu

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shredded roti or paratha (leftover or freshly made)
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup carrots, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, finely shredded
  • 1/2 cup leeks or green onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or coriander leaves for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Cooking Instructions

Prepare Roti:

  • If you don't have leftover roti, prepare or heat them according to package instructions. Once done, shred the roti into small pieces using your hands or a knife.

Stir-Fry Vegetables:

  • Heat oil in a large pan or griddle over medium-high heat. Add chopped onions, garlic, and green chili. Saut√© until the onions are translucent.

Add Vegetables:

  • Add carrots, cabbage, leeks or green onions, and chopped tomatoes to the pan. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables are tender.

Spice it Up:

  • Add curry powder, turmeric powder, chili powder, salt, and pepper to the vegetables. Mix well to coat the vegetables in the spices.

Add Shredded Roti:

  • Add the shredded roti to the pan. Mix everything together, ensuring the roti absorbs the flavors of the spices. Create a Well:

Cook Beaten Eggs:

  • Pour the beaten eggs into the well. Allow them to cook partially before scrambling and mixing them with the rest of the ingredients.

Continue Mixing:

  • Mix the eggs and the rest of the ingredients thoroughly until well combined. Cook for a few more minutes until everything is heated through.

Garnish and Serve:

  • Garnish with fresh cilantro or coriander leaves. Serve hot with lime wedges on the side.
